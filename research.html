Research Projects

Tailored to a speicific scientific query and run by undergraduate research assistants, the following projects investigate the dynamic interplay between neural, glial, and vascular systems that shape cortical function.

Simultaneous EEG, Optical Imaging, & Behavioral Assessments

Using transgenic animals we are able to simultaneously capture and cross-correlate neural activity, vascular fluctuations, and behavioral output.

Through wide-field optical imaging (Ca/CBV) and EEG, our experiments measure how blood distribution and neural activation synchronize across cortical regions. This work enhances our understanding of brain energy dynamics and informs translational models of neurological and psychiatric disorders.

We have created a unified framework for analyzing large-scale cortical dynamics. Our pipelines include automated motion tracking, state segmentation, and cross-modal time-series correlation.

These tools are being developed as open-source resources for the neuroscience community.

In Utero FASD treatment

This project aims to determine if reservatrol, administered in utero, can mitigate the onset and development of FASDs. Using transgenic mice and our tripartite system we seek to provide a comprehensive analysis and potential therapeutic route for the treatment of prenatal alcohol exposure.

Blocking Alcohols Cognitive & Behavioral Impairment

This project aims to uncover and block a potential supplementary pathway for alcohols entry into the brain. Using transgenic rats and our tripartite system we seek to provide a comprehensive analysis highlighting a potential therapeutic target to block alcohol induced cogntive and behavioral impairment.

Side Quest: An educational tool to teach students about BCI's

Brain computer interfaces are sweeping the field of neuroscience. This projects aims to bring that technology into the classroom to allow students to gain a hands-on experience planning and conducting, non-invasive, experiments that will allow them to use EMG or EEG to control actions on a computer, or other electronic devices. Through this experience, students will gain insight into how technology paired with brain/neural activity can improve the quality of life for indvidiuals with neurlogical impariment.
