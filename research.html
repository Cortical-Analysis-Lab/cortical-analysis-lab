Research

Our lab investigates the dynamic relationships between neural activity, glial signaling, and vascular function — exploring how these interactions drive behavior and brain health.

Research Areas

Neurovascular Coupling: Understanding how neuronal firing and hemodynamic responses interact under different behavioral and physiological states using optical imaging and EEG synchronization.

Cortical State Dynamics: Quantifying large-scale oscillatory and metabolic changes that underlie transitions between alertness, rest, and anesthesia.

Behavioral Correlates: Relating multimodal brain signals to spatial exploration, decision-making, and anxiety-related behavior through advanced tracking paradigms such as the open field, zero maze, and object recognition tests.

Methods & Technology

Our integrated pipeline combines widefield optical imaging, EEG acquisition, and behavioral video tracking in synchronized, timestamp-aligned systems. All data are analyzed using in-house MATLAB and Python tools for motion correction, signal normalization, and multimodal correlation mapping.

Recent technical developments include custom Nicla Vision–based imaging modules for wireless acquisition, optimized LED excitation arrays, and open-source EEG–behavior analysis toolkits for signal classification and state segmentation.

Future Directions

Our upcoming work will extend multimodal measurements to awake, freely behaving animals to study neural–vascular integration during naturalistic tasks. These studies aim to establish predictive biomarkers of brain states relevant to neurodevelopmental and psychiatric disorders.
