Research

Our lab studies the dynamic interplay between neural, glial, and vascular systems that shape cortical function. Using multimodal imaging, electrophysiology, and behavioral tracking, we aim to uncover how brain states emerge from complex biological coordination.

Neurovascular Coupling

We investigate how neuronal activity drives vascular responses at multiple scales. Through wide-field optical imaging (Ca/CBV) and EEG, our experiments measure how oxygenation and neural activation synchronize across cortical regions. This work enhances understanding of brain energy dynamics, and informs translational models of neurological and psychiatric disorders.

Behavioral Correlates of Cortical Activity

Our behavioral tracking systems link real-time movement and decision-making with neural patterns. We analyze open-field exploration, zero maze, and object interaction tests to quantify the neural signatures of anxiety, curiosity, and habituation. Multimodal Data Integration

By combining wide-field optical imaging, EEG, and computational models, we create a unified framework for analyzing large-scale cortical dynamics. Our pipelines include automated motion tracking, state segmentation, and cross-modal time-series correlation. These tools are being developed as open-source frameworks for the neuroscience community.

Translational and Therapeutic Insights

We aim to connect fundamental neuroscience to clinical outcomes. By mapping cortical biomarkers of plasticity, stress response, and altered neurovascular signaling, we hope to advance translational research relevant to developmental, behavioral, and neurodegenerative disorders. Our long-term goal is to build predictive and therapeutic models for brain health and recovery.
